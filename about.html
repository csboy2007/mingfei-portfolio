My path from
a programmer
2 a designer

Hello -my name is Mingfei Bi. Over the course of my ten years working as a professional programmer, my experiences in the IT industry have made me passionate about the importance of UX design.

In 2001, I became a programmer and joined one of the top food companies, Uni-President China. At that time, there was no specific position for a UX designer in China - all the UX jobs were assigned to programmers, which gave me my first exposure to design. I still remember interviewing a high-level account manager. She told me: “the new system needs reputation.” What does it mean for a computer system to earn a reputation? At the time I thought it was the trust of my clients, but now I know this is built and communicated through good user experience.

In 2007, I came to the US for my master’s degree in Computer Science. After two years, I graduated understanding how to build systems in new coding languages. Furthermore, I had begun to learn how to build software with 'reputation.’ Since 2009, I have worked for two small companies. My team didn't have the budget to support a UX designer joining the company, so I took on most of the UX jobs voluntarily. I have since had the opportunity to work with my users, design new systems, facilitate kick off meetings, and execute user training as a UX designer, all while using my background in CS to collaborate with the developer team as a developer.

In 2017, I attended a UX designer training course. It was an influential course that taught me the ins and outs of building UX team, and motivated me to pursue this path full-time professionally.
